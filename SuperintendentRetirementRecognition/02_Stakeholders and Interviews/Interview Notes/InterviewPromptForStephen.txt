I’d like you to help create a personal and professional history transcript in your own words.

Purpose:
This is an oral-history style interview about my life, career, leadership, and memories of Broadalbin-Perth. The goal is to preserve personal recollections, stories, names, turning points, lessons, and institutional history while the memories are still fresh.

Tone:
Please make this conversational, reflective, and honest. Approximate dates are fine. Use first person. Include stories, people, places, and memories where they help explain the answer. Do not make it sound like a polished biography unless that naturally fits the answer.

Format:
Ask me one question at a time. After I answer, ask the next question. If my answer suggests an interesting follow-up, ask one brief follow-up before moving on. At the end, produce a clean transcript that I can copy and paste, organized by question and answer.

Please begin by explaining that you will ask up to 20 questions, one at a time, and that the final product will be a transcript.

Questions to ask:

1. What are your earliest memories of growing up in Broadalbin, and what was the community like when you were young?

2. What role did school play in your life as a child and teenager?

3. Who were the teachers, coaches, administrators, family members, neighbors, or community members who most influenced you?

4. What do you remember about being part of the Broadalbin Central School Class of 1987, the last class before the merger with Perth?

5. Looking back, how do you think the Broadalbin-Perth merger shaped the identity of the district and community? 

6. What led you toward physical education, teaching, and a career in schools?

7. What do you remember about your college years at Fulton-Montgomery, SUNY Cortland, and SUNY Albany, and how did those experiences shape you?

8. What was it like to return to Broadalbin-Perth as a physical education teacher?

9. What did your early teaching years teach you about students, motivation, opportunity, and school culture?

10. What brought you back to Broadalbin-Perth as primary school principal, and what did that role teach you?

11. How did your move into district-level leadership change your understanding of how schools work?

12. What do you remember most about becoming superintendent of your hometown district?

13. How would you describe your educational philosophy?

14. What major programs, initiatives, facilities improvements, or district changes from your tenure do you think are most important to remember?

15. Do you have some funny stories you’d be willing to share (from any period)? Who made you laugh and kept you going?

16. What were the most difficult periods of leadership, and what did they teach you about decision-making, communication, and trust?

17. Who are the people - board members, administrators, teachers, staff, students, families, or community members - who deserve to be remembered as part of the district’s story?

18. Outside of your formal school roles, how have family, community service, music, and everyday life shaped who you are?

19. You’ve pushed yourself into challenges like Iron Man and riskier military exercises. Why do you think that is, and what have you learned through these experiences?

20. When future students, staff, or community members look back on this period in Broadalbin-Perth history, what do you hope they understand?

After the final answer:
Please produce a clean transcript I can copy and paste. Use this structure:

Title: Personal and Professional History Interview

Introductory note:
A brief paragraph explaining that this transcript captures personal recollections and reflections in the interviewee’s own words.

Then format each item as:

Question 1:
[question]

Answer:
[my answer]

If follow-up questions were asked, include them under the original question.

At the end, add a short section titled “Names, Dates, and Items to Verify” listing any people, dates, places, programs, documents, photographs, or events that may need later confirmation.
