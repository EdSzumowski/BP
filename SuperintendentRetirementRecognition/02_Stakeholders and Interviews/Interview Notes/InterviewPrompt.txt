I’d like you to help create a short oral-history style reflection about Stephen Tomlinson and his years at Broadalbin-Perth.

Purpose:
This is meant to capture memories, stories, examples, and personal reflections from people who worked closely with Stephen or knew his leadership well. The goal is to preserve character, context, district history, and the human side of his work.

Tone:
Please make this conversational, specific, and story-based. It does not need to sound formal. The best answers will include examples, memories, names, moments, challenges, or small details that show what Stephen was like as a person and leader.

Format:
Ask me one question at a time. After I answer, ask the next question. If my answer suggests an important follow-up, ask one brief follow-up before moving on. At the end, produce a clean transcript that I can copy and paste, organized by question and answer.

Please begin by explaining that you will ask up to 10 questions, one at a time, and that the final product will be a transcript.

Questions to ask:

1. How did you first come to know or work with Stephen Tomlinson?

2. What are your strongest memories of him as an educator, administrator, superintendent, colleague, or community member?

3. What personal qualities best describe Stephen, and what stories show those qualities?

4. What do you think he cared about most in his work?

5. Can you describe a time when you saw his leadership make a difference for students, staff, families, or the community?

6. What programs, changes, traditions, facilities, or district improvements do you most associate with his years of leadership?

7. Were there difficult moments when you saw his character, judgment, or commitment tested?

8. What do people outside the district office or leadership circle sometimes not see or understand about him?

9. Are there funny, warm, surprising, or especially human stories about Stephen that should not be lost?

10. When people look back on Stephen’s place in Broadalbin-Perth history, what do you hope they remember?

After the final answer:
Please produce a clean transcript I can copy and paste. Use this structure:

Title: Reflection on Stephen Tomlinson’s Service and Leadership

Introductory note:
A brief paragraph explaining who the speaker is, how they know Stephen, and the general period they are reflecting on.

Then format each item as:

Question 1:
[question]

Answer:
[my answer]

If follow-up questions were asked, include them under the original question.

At the end, add a short section titled “Names, Dates, and Items to Verify” listing any people, dates, places, programs, documents, photographs, or events that may need later confirmation.
