# Venue Research Memo - Stephen Tomlinson Retirement Celebration

Prepared for: Broadalbin-Perth superintendent retirement recognition planning  
Event target: invitation-based retirement celebration, spring 2027  
Guest target: up to 200  
Tone: dignified, warm, adult-focused, school-community appropriate  
Alcohol posture: adult beverages may be available, but district funds should not pay for alcohol.

## Important notes on source quality

This memo uses current public web information found during initial research. Public venue pages and third-party venue directories are useful for capacity, contact, and rough package signals, but they are not substitutes for written venue quotes. Pricing, menus, food and beverage minimums, rental charges, service charges, taxes, staffing charges, and availability should be confirmed directly in writing.

## Working assumptions

- Broadalbin-Perth Junior/Senior High School is the travel-time anchor.
- Thursday, Friday, and Saturday are preferred, with Sunday considered if pricing or availability is meaningfully better.
- The best fit is not necessarily the cheapest venue. This event should feel respectful, celebratory, and not bar-centered.
- District facilities remain a last-resort fallback.
- A short program with speakers is required. Strong preference for microphone, podium, screen/projector or TV, and basic audio support.

## Last-resort district facility options

District facilities should remain fallback options only if venue quotes are unavailable, too costly, or inconsistent with public-fund expectations.

Possible facility formats:

- High school cafeteria/commons reception with district-approved caterer
- Auditorium ceremony followed by cafeteria/commons dessert reception
- Gymnasium banquet using rented tables/chairs/pipe-and-drape
- Hybrid: formal program in auditorium, offsite smaller dinner for invited adults

Pros:

- Lowest direct venue cost
- Highest symbolic connection to Stephen's work
- Strong A/V control if auditorium is used
- Convenient for staff, board, and school community

Cons:

- May feel less adult/special
- Requires staff labor, custodial support, security, rentals, insurance, food service/catering coordination
- Alcohol likely should be excluded entirely on school property
- Could blur ceremony and official school functions

## Alcohol structure notes

Recommended structures, in order:

1. Cash bar paid directly by guests to venue.
2. Guest-paid consumption bar with no district invoice line for alcohol.
3. Drink tickets funded by a private/non-district source, with food and non-alcohol costs invoiced separately to the district or event account.
4. Beer/wine-only cash bar if full bar feels too bar-centered.
5. No-host bar in a separate room or discreet side station.
6. No alcohol, if public-fund optics or counsel guidance suggest avoiding it.

Contract language to request:

- District-paid invoice must exclude alcohol.
- Alcohol charges must be billed separately to guests or a non-district host.
- No open bar paid with district funds.
- Venue must provide insurance, licensed bartenders, and compliance with NYS liquor rules.
- Event layout should not center the bar visually or socially.

## Preferred event format recommendation

Best default: buffet-style dinner with a short formal program.

Reason: A buffet dinner gives the event enough gravity for a superintendent retirement recognition without requiring a formal plated gala. It also gives a clearer program moment and seating plan for speeches/video.

Best lower-cost alternative: heavy hors d'oeuvres cocktail reception with limited seating plus a brief program.

Caution: For a school-district event, a cocktail reception must be designed so it does not feel like a bar event. Use high-top tables only as accents, provide enough seating for older guests, keep the bar to the side, and schedule the program early.

## Suggested narrowing process

1. Confirm target date window and preferred day-of-week hierarchy.
2. Send inquiry email to the first 8 venues.
3. Request two separate quotes from each venue: buffet dinner and heavy hors d'oeuvres reception.
4. Require alcohol-separated quote structure.
5. Ask each venue to hold or identify 2-3 available spring 2027 dates.
6. Score each venue on capacity, tone, travel, cost, A/V, alcohol separation, accessibility, parking, and contract simplicity.
7. Conduct site visits for top 3-4.
8. Select one preferred venue and one backup.
9. Route draft contract/payment/alcohol structure through district business office and counsel before deposit.
10. Revisit the invite count before final contract minimums are signed.

## Best current working recommendation

If the goal is the strongest balance of community fit, setting, travel distance, and program capability, start with Lanzi's on the Lake. It is local, lake-centered, large enough for 200, and publicly identifies program equipment. If Lanzi's is unavailable, too expensive, or not appropriate contractually, the next strongest practical options appear to be The Bridge Walk, Waterford Banquets, Excelsior Springs, and Holiday Inn Saratoga Springs. If the board wants a more formal/premium gala feel, add Saratoga National and Key Hall to the top tier.
